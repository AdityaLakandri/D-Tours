Momos

These are probably the most popular local snack food in Darjeelingpf Tibetan origin. They are dumplings made with flour dough and stuffed with either meat or vegetables. The nonveg momos have either chicken or pork as stuffing, the veggie ones will have cabbage, squash and other vegetables. Momos can be steamed or fried. The more popular ones are the steamed ones.Momos are served with clear soup and tomato chutney.

Thukpa

Another very popular Tibetan food id Thukpa. It is essentially a hot noodle soup mixed with meat,eggs, vegetables etc ans served in a large bowl. Although thukpa is more like a starter, for an average eater,this can easily fill up the stomach. Note that Wai Wai is also another similar dish prepared with noodles and served dry(hakka) or with soup base.

Alu Mimi

Alu Dum is a typical Nepali and Bengali snack item or a side dish.It is prepared by boiling cut potatoes in a thick gravy and miing dried red chilli powder in it along with onion slices, few cloves of garlic,some mustard oil and sometimes even red color. it is mixed with mimi(ready to eat noodles) or bhujia to make it a tempting snack.

Traditional Nepali Thali

Thali means plate. Nepali thali comprises of a full meal served on a plate which comprises of several items like daal(lentils cooked in a soup),bhaat (boiled rice),tarkari(assortment of vegetable curries served on small bowls), chutney or achaar(pickles),curd,papad and a sweet item.You can compliment this with chicken other meat currry.

Dalle(Hot Pickle)

If you savor the taste of hot chilli,then this popular local pickle is for you. It is prepared with round red chilli, mustard oil ,salt and spices. Some acidic acid is added for preservation. The pickle is sold in glass bottles. They look like smll red balls suspended in a reddish liquid....very hot but delicious. This are also used for making chutneys of momos.

Sael Roti

You won't usually get this item of Nepali origin in restaurants.But if you happen to get friendly with alocal, ask him or her to invite you for Sael Rotis. These are rotis(flat round breads) made from rice paste and then fried deeply. Tastes quite nice but you should typically have it with butter,cheese or chutney.

Gundruk

This a typical Nepali item which is prepared at local homes.You won't usually get it at any restaurant. So again you need a local friend to try this out. It is actually not a dish by itself. It's prepared for flavour and typical strong smell. green leaves of plants like mustard,radish etc are left for fermenting over one or two days. Then they are dried naturally under the sun and it becomes Gundruk(with strong acidic smell).The shredded dry leaves can be then mixed with vegetablesor prepared with onions and radish to give it a unique flavoour and taste.

Darjeeling Tea

Well this is something that has put Darjeeling firmly into world map of tea and this is the place where you get the original 'Champaign of Teas'. Unless you have a cup of authetic Darjeeling,chat with the tea room owner, listen some music and enjoy the wonderful viewof the valley, you would miss out somethimg that you will regret for long.
